Astro Pi Build

Start by understanding the hardware you will be working with and what it takes to build a flight case.

  • Get familiar with the Astro Pi hardware. astro-pi.org
  • Research the Sense HAT — work through the getting started guide. projects.raspberrypi.org
  • Look into what is needed to 3D print the flight case housing. projects.raspberrypi.org

Pico2 Navigation Robots

Get to know the robot kit, its components, and the Pico 2 controller that runs it all.

  • Explore all the resources from Yahboom, the company that makes the kit. www.yahboom.net
  • Organize the tutorials — figure out what order makes sense and what covers what.
  • Start documenting each component of the Pico2 robot. What does each part do? category.yahboom.net
  • Learn about the Pico 2 controller itself. www.raspberrypi.com

LED Making

Start with the fundamentals of controlling LEDs. This is the foundation for everything you will build.

  • Work through the LED basics workshop — introduction through first exercises. hackathon.p4labs.io

Scratch Helpers

Your job is to support Grade 6 students working on Scratch projects. Start by understanding what they are building.

  • Familiarize yourself with the Grade 6 Scratch projects on the dedicated website. Note that each team has their own hub for collecting information and feedback. jtrudeau.github.io
  • Create a Scratch account if you do not have one already. We will make at least one game, quest, or activity that complements the Grade 6 projects. scratch.mit.edu
